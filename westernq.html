Overview

As a part of the WITS Ada Mentorship Program, I designed a mobile application that allows students at Western University to search how long the lines are at various eateries around campus. For many, it is a common issue to encounter long line-ups at the eateries on campus, especially in the University Community Centre. This app design aims to address this inconveniencce rather than having students guess how long the line may be so that they can calculate if they will be late to class or not.


Problem Statement

"How might we improve the waiting experience at eateries?"

Iterations

Then came the wireframes. After putting together my initial batch of wireframes, I sent them out to the same users I conducted interviews on to test, collecting their feedback through a standardized Google Forms along with a screen recording of their test. This process led me to make iterations on iterations, and really opened up my eyes to how my design decisions initially may not have been as intuitive as I thought they were upon feedback. After continuously iterating and testing, I went on to create a hi-fidelity mockup of the latest iteration.

The First Wireframe

Layout of screens I THOUGHT were needed. Little interaction at all.

Second Iteration

I learned (thanks to my mentor) that wireframes should be a lot more dynamic! Added more copy, and more interactions such as onboarding screens, expanding eatery cards instead of opening a new page, a favourites page, and different setting permissions.

Hi-Fi Iteration

Used orange-toned colours to incite hunger based off of colour psychology, refined the search, moved filtering options to the main page.

Reflections

This was the first project where I conducted market research and collected insights from others to improve my designs, compared to just doing what I thought looked good. In doing so, I discovered the value of testing; sending out prototypes for others to test really gave me fresh perspectives on what was intuitive and what was not. Each subsequent iteration was an improvement from the last, which felt really rewarding! In retrospect, I would spend a lot more time on the research process to fully try to understand the problem and potential users, as well as on the testing phase as that became a bit more rushed towards the end as the showcase to present the project as approaching. Overall however, I'm proud of what I have done and in the future I hope to be able to continue iterating on the designs of the interface and incorporate more user feedback.
